Our Unique Practical Teaching & Learning Approach

The Early Learning Program

Helps with language and literacy. It encourages the child to:

createCommunicate his/her experiences, ideas and feelings by speaking.

createListen with understanding, follow directions, conversations and stories.

createExhibit interest in reading.


The motor skills room

Provides the child with opportunities to :

createExhibit curiosity, creativity, self direction and persistence in learning situations.

createEngage in activities that he/she selects or creates

createDemonstrate self direction in use of materials

createMake independent decisions about what materials to work with and will get and use the materials he/she needs.

The classroom discussions

on various topics and themes help the child to:

createExpress wonder, ask questions and seek answers about the natural world..

createRecognize and solve problems through active exploration, including trial and error and interacting with peers and adults.

createOrganize and express his/her understanding of common properties and attributes of things.


The outdoor play area

The outdoor play area gives the child opportunities to :

createEngage in a wide variety of gross-motor activities that are selected by him/her and initiated by the teacher

createUse a variety of materials that promote eye-hand coordination and small-muscle development.

createDemonstrate spatial awareness in gross-motor activities.

Assessment

create Assessment is defined as the process of observing, recording and documenting what .your child does, knows and understands.
create Throughout the year, we oversee the child and document his/her progress. Our knowledge of your child helps us to plan an appropriate and challenging curriculum that is tailored to meet his/her strengths, needs and interests.
create Under the direction and supervision of the centre head, all staff at the preschool are involved in the assessment of children. They receive on-going training and have access to teaching resources to support their understanding.
create Progress is observed and documented in the following developmental and academic content areas: Cognitive, Language, Social, Emotional, Physical, Literacy, Math, Science, Social Studies, Art, Music and Dance.
create The children are assessed three times a year, in August, December and March. Assessments are based on utilization of teaching strategies that include on-going observations and samples of classroom work. Kindergarten students go through informal and stress-free tests for assessments.
create Parent-teacher conferences are typically conducted at least thrice during the school year to encourage parental involvement and discuss your child’s current performance and progress.
create All gathered information is kept confidential.
